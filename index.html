Four quiet jobs

Sentinel stays out of the way until something looks wrong. Then it does its work in large type and short sentences.

01

It watches

Using the message screening Apple opened in iOS 27, Sentinel checks incoming texts against known scam patterns: the fake delivery fee, the impostor grandchild, the bank alert that isn't from a bank, and more added over time.

02

It explains

No jargon, no red sirens. A calm card says what this is and why it looks wrong. On newer iPhones the explanation is written on the spot for that exact message. On older ones it comes from carefully written playbooks. Protection is identical either way.

03

It offers one safe step

Block the sender with guided help. Or ask a question. Or say "this one's actually fine," which Sentinel remembers. There is always a way to double-check for free: share any suspicious text or screenshot to Sentinel and get a verdict in seconds.

04

It tells the family

If your loved one opts in, you get a short digest: "Dad blocked a scam today. No action needed." You see what kind of scam it was. You never see their messages, and they know exactly what you can see, because we showed them during setup.

Fair questions

Does Sentinel read my loved one's messages?

Screening happens inside iOS, on their phone, using Apple's Trust Insights framework. Nothing is uploaded. When Sentinel writes an explanation, that happens on the device too. The single exception: if your loved one catches a scam we missed, they can choose to send us that one message to make Sentinel better. It asks every time, in large print.

Which iPhones does it work on?

Any iPhone that runs iOS 27, which means iPhone 12 and newer. Recent models write custom explanations on the device. Older models use our pre-written playbooks for each scam type. Detection quality is the same on both.

What does my loved one have to do?

After the five-minute setup, nothing. Sentinel includes a practice run so they see what a warning looks like before a real one arrives. When one does, the card tells them the one safe thing to do next.

What about phone calls and email?

Texts and shared screenshots come first, and a screenshot covers most scam email and voicemail transcripts too. On calls, honesty matters: iOS does not let any app listen in on a live conversation, so nobody can truthfully sell live call protection. Sentinel ships what is real today. Known scam numbers get blocked or labeled before the phone ever rings, and setup helps your family agree on a private code word, which defeats the impostor call no matter how convincing the voice sounds. When Apple opens live screening with the same privacy guarantees, we will adopt it.
